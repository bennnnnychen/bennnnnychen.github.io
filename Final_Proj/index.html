Introduction

This project aims to visualize the HouseShareProhibitedBuildingsList from Chicago. It is the Building information from City of Chicago data portal. The dataset can be accessed here. This is a very large dataset of 2268 x 16. It lists all the building information as rows, with different properties as columns, which include information like district numbers, logitude, latitude and etc.

Original dataset

This is the dataset used in this project. As shown below, the dataset contains important information like number of units and recorded date. In addition, longitude and latitude can be used to visualize the distribution of the buildings in each area.

Central visualization

First of all, we will take a look at the distribution of the number of units in each recorded year. Since most 90% of the “Recorded Date” is within the range of 2015 and 2020, we will filter out the years outside of this range to make the graph more sparse.

The distribution can be further inspected by using the buttons below. By clicking each option, the original chart will be colored by the property selected.

Color by:

It can be seen that most of the data locates in the range of 2015 to 2021. Moreover, most of the units are located in the direction of North and West, and most of the street types are Avenue.

Contextual visualization

Here we will take a look at the distribution of the buildings according to their latitude and longitude. Below is a picture generated by the website visualization generator. The X and Y axes are Latitude and Longitude. This picture will serve as a supplement for the building information.

We also want to know what is the relationship between the location and number of units. Therefore, we can generate another visualization from the website visualization generator about the Latitude and Number of Units.

It can be seen that most of the blocks have number of units below 500. However, around latitude 41, there are some outliers having a very high number of units.
